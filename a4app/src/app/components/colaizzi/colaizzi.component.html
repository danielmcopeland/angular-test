The problem with Colaizzi's Method

Here is a comparison of two methods of data analysis taken from an opinion survey on salary ranges for music therapists. Responses came from health administrators and health administration students. Colaizzi's Method identifies themes in responses and compares them based on their frequency. Since this method sometimes pulls multiple pieces of data from individual responses, some responses become weighted more heavily than others. This is effective for qualitative data and finding themes in written responses, but can cause inaccuracies when dealing with relational data (especially numerical data).
You can see how these innaccuracies affect data visualization by adding your own data.


For a great example of the differences, clear all the entries and input 49000-59000.


Notice that while the majority of the input (49k-59k) falls under the range of 50000-59999, Colaizzi's Method treats ANY ranges that the input falls into EQUALLY. The modified method however adds weight to the portion of the input that falls in each range.
